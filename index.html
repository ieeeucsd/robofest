What is Robofest?

  • Visit our Facebook Page

The IEEE Student Branch at UC San Diego has been hosting the California Micromouse and Grand PrIEEE competitions every year around May for the past 8 years and 9 years respectively. Both competitions were held in different consecutive weekends and IEEE has helped organize and run the competitions here at UC San Diego. This year, the IEEE student branch would like to combine both project competitions into a single weekend to be a celebration of robotics projects and extend our invitation to students not only at UCSD, but students at other schools to create the most premier robotics competition in the region. We would also like to partner with nearby robotics and technology companies in addition to other UCSD student organizations to observe the competition. Robofest is intended to be for students, by students to be a collaborative, fun experience.

Learn more below about the competitions we will be hosting this Robofest!

What is Micromouse?

Micromouse is an annual IEEE UCSD event where teams compete with small robot mice in an attempt to be the fastest to solve a 16x16 maze.

The California Micromouse competition (CAMM) has been around since its introduction by IEEE in 1977. Our competition today features a 16x16 grid maze and much fiercer competition, as technology has come a long way!

What is Grand PRIEEE?

Grand PRIEEE is an engineering design competition in which teams compete to build and race small autonomous line-following vehicles.

The Grand PrIEEE competition (formerly Viacar) involves following a track consisting of a wire carrying a 100mA rms 75kHz sinusoidal signal taped to the floor. Both the wire and the white tape provide opportunities to track the course, typically via either induction or computer vision. Given that the typical hobbyist remote control car today can achieve significant speed, this makes the ability to optimize a tracking algorithm critical to success.

What is RoboCup Soccer: Small Size League?

RoboCup is essentially fully autonomous robots playing soccer with two teams and six robots each.

The small size league, or F180 league is one of the oldest RoboCup Soccer leagues. We are excited to be bringing this competition to Robofest this year. The six robots on each team must adhere to the F180 league rules where the robots must fit in a 180mm diameter circle and be no taller than 15cm. Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to pay to attend the event?

No! It is completely free!

What if I am not competing? Should I still attend?

We are hosting many workshops on the first day, you can definitely attend to those to develop your skills. You can also come to the watch the competition, and support your college team! We just ask that you fill out the RSVP form!

Can I bring kids to this event?

Yes! We are hosting a robo clinic on the first day which is a workshop about robotics designed for kids. It is hosted by First Robotics.
